ğŸ“ğŸ“Œ Important Logistical Notes

  • Visa Requirement: As a Spanish national, a Visa (Type L - Tourist or Type M - Business) is mandatory for entry into China. The 15-day visa-free transit rule does not apply to this multi-city itinerary. Apply for a multiple-entry or long-duration single-entry visa at least 3 months in advance.
  • Currency & Payment: The local currency is the Chinese Yuan Renminbi (CNY/RMB). While major cities accept international cards (Visa/Mastercard) at larger establishments, cash is essential for smaller vendors. Mobile payment apps (WeChat Pay/Alipay) are dominant; set these up to link to your foreign card before arrival for seamless travel.
  • Internet Access: Google services (Maps, Gmail, Search) and popular social media are blocked (Great Firewall). A reliable VPN is essential for accessing these during your trip. Purchase and test a VPN before departure. Local SIM cards are available but require passport registration.
  • Time Zone: China operates on a single time zone (Beijing Time, UTC+8). This is 7 hours ahead of Barcelona (CET). Expect significant jet lag on arrival.
  • Internal Travel: This itinerary relies heavily on domestic flights for efficiency between far-flung cities (Beijing-Xi'an, Xi'an-Guilin, Guilin-Shanghai). Book these well in advance, and allow ample time for check-in.
  • Bookings: Essential activities like the Great Wall tour, Terracotta Army, and Shanghai Acrobatic Show should be pre-booked online to save time and ensure entry, especially during peak season (which Mar is approaching).
